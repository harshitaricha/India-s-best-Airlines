1. IndiGo


Indigo ranked 1st in the list of Best Airlines in India. This is an Indian low-cost airline headquartered at Gurgaon, Haryana, India.
The airline was founded in 2006 as a private company by Rahul Bhatia of InterGlobe Enterprises and Rakesh Gangwal, a United States-based Indian.
The airline took delivery of its 1st aircraft in July 2006 and started operations a month later in 20016. The airline became the largest Indian carrier as per the passenger market share in 2012.
The airline went Limited in November 2015 and issue share to the public Indigo is the biggest airline in India by passengers carried and fleet size, it has a maximum 48.1% domestic market share as of June 2019. Indigo is also the largest individual Asian low-cost carrier in terms of jet fleet size and passengers carried.
Advertisement Indigo is also ranked seventh-largest carrier in Asia with over 46 million passengers carried in 2017. As of now, the airline operates flights to 79 destinations which include 58 domestic and 29 international. The maximum flights of Indigo Airlines are operated from Indira Gandhi International airport, Delhi
